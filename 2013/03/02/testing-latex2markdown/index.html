Theorem 1 (Euclid)

There are infinitely many primes.

Proof

Suppose that are all of the primes. Let and let $p$ be a prime dividing $P$.

Then $p$ can not be any of $p_i$, for otherwise $p$ would divide the difference , which is impossible. So this prime $p$ is still another prime, and cannot be all of the primes.
