Notes on the Null Space Property

(Based on readings in the References)

  1. Why does min∥x∥1 s.t. Ax=b result in sparse signals whereas min∥x∥2 s.t. Ax=b is less likely to yield sparse signals ?

  2. According to this 1, why is the following true ?

    A sensing matrix Am×n has the null space property of order s if and only if
    for all ∥x0∥≤s, x0 is the unique minimizer of

    min∥x∥1 s.t. Ax=Ax0

    • Let v be any element from the null space of A, v∈(A)∖{0}.

    • Suppose the null space of the sensing matrix A contain at least one sparse signal over the support S. Then the cardinality of v over the set Sc is small i.e. ∥vSc∥1≤∥vS∥1. Thus, the null space property is violated since a non-zero sparse vector cannot satisfy ∥vS∥1<∥vSc∥1.

    • Any vector v=vS in the null space satisfies Ax=A(x+v)=b and thus belongs in the feasible set. For any minimizer x0, one can construct another signal x0−vSc that also solves the problem min∥x∥1 s.t. Ax=Ax0.
    • Thus we no longer have a unique minimizer x0
